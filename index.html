SeaLife

SeaLife is a personal project which aims to form as many people as possible to the protection of the marine fauna and flora. Protection of the marine fauna and flora is necessary for us to live. Through photosynthesis, phytoplankton produces a large amount of oxygen necessary for life in the water, but also, thanks to the gas exchange to the ocean surface, it provides 2/3 of the oxygen in the air our planet, the last 1/3 from plant continents. Contrary to popular belief, the largest producer of oxygen on Earth is not the forest, but phytoplankton that brings the atmosphere more oxygen than all the united world's forests, Amazonian forest range. The atmosphere is rich with 21% oxygen. This vital element was produced mainly by phytoplankton and photosynthetic bacteria to the early history of the Earth through photosynthesis. This aquatic oxygen released into the air allowed the formation of the ozone layer (which contains three oxygen atoms) in the upper atmosphere. Without this protection against ultraviolet radiation from the sun, ever green plants could not conquer continents, there are 600 million years, followed closely by animals.

What can I do to protect the marine environment ?

Simple gesture

You can participate in the protection of marine fauna and flora through simple everyday actions. It is also very important to show these gestures to the children so they can make the best possible habits early in their lives.
